Orecchiette con Ragù di Maiale

Author: Kyle Whitcomb

Home

I created this recipe as an attempt to capture all of the intense meaty flavor of an all-day slow-simmered ragù but with the ease and speed of a pressure cooker. Sometimes you just want that Sunday pasta on a Wednesday.

This recipe takes all of those same flavors and ingredients and concentrats them using the power of a pressure cooker. You can use any pressure cooker you have, but I find electronic pressure cookers like the Instant Pot to be both easiesr and with more consistent results than their stove-top counterparts.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb De Cecco Orecchiette (1 box)
  • 2 large cans whole San Marzano tomatoes
  • 3 lbs pork shoulder
  • 1 large carrot
  • 1 celery stalk
  • 1 large shallot or small yellow onion
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • EVOO
  • Dry white wine
  • Italian seasoning
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Red pepper flakes

Directions

  1. Grate or very finely dice carrots, celery, and onions. Add olive oil to the pressure cooker on low saut√© setting, add soffritto, season with salt, and cook to soften. After a few minutes, add garlic and continue to cook. Season further with Italian seasoning and red pepper flakes.
  2. Deglaze with white wine and cook for another few minutes to cook off the alcohol and reduce.
  3. Add both cans of tomatoes, pork shoulder, and salt and pepper as needed.
  4. Pressure cook for 60 minutes with a quick pressure release.
  5. Reduce sauce in pressure cooker or stovetop until desired consistency is reached.
  6. Cook orecchiette to al dente following package directions.
  7. Mix cooked pasta and sauce on the stove or in a mixing bowl to combine.
  8. Top with grated Parmigiano Reggiano and Pecorino Romano.
